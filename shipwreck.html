shipwreck.html

I am moved, again by public domain and creative commons.

Here is the ocean, recorded across countries.

Before now, I'd never thought about what the ocean might sound like in different places. But it is different, isn't it?

suffolk, England

Source: nickmaysoundmusic, "Felixstowe_beach_waves_close_stones_seagulls_stereo.wav", freesound.org, 2 September 2021, https://freesound.org/people/nickmaysoundmusic/sounds/585622/

Maui, Hawaii

Source: deepseapro, "Black Sand Beach Walk.wav", freesound.org, 16 Jan 2017, https://freesound.org/people/deepseapro/sounds/376874/.

This one you can even hear people in.

Pattaya, Chon Buri, Thailand

Source: kjjjaz, "20161230 Kijjaz - Pattaya Beach - Far 01", freesound.org, 31 Dec 2016, https://freesound.org/people/kijjaz/sounds/372021/.

Now if you look at this ASCII here, and you play one of the sounds, you can sort of know what it was like today for me. Maybe you might imagine me in Suffolk, or Maui, or Pattaya.

It'd be a lie. I've never been to any of these places. The people who recorded these are gone.
